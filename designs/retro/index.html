The companies and machines that built the personal computing revolution

Big Blue

IBM Personal Computer

The machine that legitimized personal computing for business. IBM's entry into the PC market with its blue and white corporate identity changed everything.

1981
Think Different

Apple Computer

From the Apple II's beige case to the rainbow logo, Apple brought personal computing to homes with friendly design and revolutionary interfaces.

1976
Software Pioneer

Microsoft Corporation

Bill Gates and Paul Allen's software company rose to prominence in the 80s with MS-DOS, establishing the foundation for Windows and Office dominance.

1975
Home Computer

Commodore 64

The best-selling single computer model ever made. Brown case with blue accents, bringing computing and gaming to millions of households worldwide.

1982
Gaming Revolution

Atari Corporation

Red and orange branding defined the arcade era. From Pong to the 2600 console, Atari made electronic entertainment mainstream before the crash.

1972
Terminal Computing

Amstrad CPC

Green phosphor displays and integrated tape decks. Amstrad brought affordable color computing to Europe with distinctive design and practical engineering.

1984
Budget Innovation

Sinclair ZX Spectrum

Black keyboard with colorful rainbow stripe. Sir Clive Sinclair's affordable computer democratized programming and gaming across the UK.

1982
Radio Shack

TRS-80 Model I

Gray metal case and green-on-black display. Radio Shack's "Trash-80" was one of the first mass-produced personal computers for small businesses.

1977
Educational Computing

BBC Microcomputer

Red and beige design commissioned by the BBC for computer literacy. Acorn's machine educated a generation of British programmers and engineers.
